# Method

## Habituation

- <u>Independent Variable</u>: time
- <u>Dependent Variable</u>: Time to relaxation
- <u>Procedure</u>
    - Gentle touch to siphon, produces gill withdrawal
    - Repeat same touch every minute for 10-15 min
    - Progressively weaker, **shorter** withdrawal durations

## Sensitization

- <u>Independent Variable</u>:
- <u>Dependent Variable</u>: Time to relaxation
- <u>Procedure</u>
    - Gentle touch to siphon, produces gill withdrawal
    - **Aversive** (big) shock to tail (different area than the siphon)
    - Next touch, much **longer** withdrawal duration

## Classical Conditioning

- <u>Independent Variable</u>:
- <u>Dependent Variable</u>:
- <u>Procedure</u>
    - Initial
        - *CS*: light touch to the siphon, initially producing little to no withdrawal
        - *US*  `->` *UR*: non-sensitizing (not as intense) tail shock `->` strong gill withdrawal (CR)
    - Training
        - CS, US: Pair light touch with non-sensitizing tail shock
    - Result
        - *CS* `->` *CR*: light touch comes to produce a strong withdrawal

# Result

## Habituation

- <img src="https://tva1.sinaimg.cn/large/e6c9d24egy1h6ljn8xafyj20yu0rs41a.jpg" width="300">
- Short term [[Synapse#^313ebe|synaptic depression]]: reduction in **glutamate**
- Long term: sensory-motor synapses [[Pruning|pruned]] away

## Sensitization

<img src="https://tva1.sinaimg.cn/large/e6c9d24egy1h6lkclnvzmj216s0rqgoc.jpg" width="300"> <img src="https://tva1.sinaimg.cn/large/e6c9d24egy1h6lkxe0qcxj216e0tqwhm.jpg" width="300">

> [[Serotonin]] `->` Inhibition of $K^+$ channel `->` Broadened (long time) [[action potential]] `->` $Ca^{++}$ channel open longer `->` more [[neurotransmitter]] released `->` Increased response
- Short term:
    - Activates [[Neuron#^308616|Interneurons]] that release [[Serotonin]]
    - [[neuromodulators|Modulates]] sensory neurons to release **more** neurotransmitter (glutamate) on next activation
- Long term: new sensory-motor synapses are **added**

## Classical Conditioning Result

<img src="https://tva1.sinaimg.cn/large/008vxvgGgy1h7jtqxtzmgj31aq0t8tcc.jpg" width="300"> <img src="https://tva1.sinaimg.cn/large/008vxvgGgy1h7jtr2ws5bj319g0u0wic.jpg" width="300">

- Activity-dependent enhancement
    - `CS `– weak sensory activation that primes the sensory neuron, but **causes no further change**
    - `US` – serotonin release, but **too weak to cause sensitization**
    - `CS+US` – **primed** sensory neuron responds to even weak level of serotonin, its synapses become facilitated (become [[Long-term potentiation|stronger]])
    - Combination of activity-based **priming + serotonin** modulation strengthens the `CS` pathway, so that future touches cause more robust withdrawals (`CR`)
    - These changes only last about $0.5 s$ - if the US is too late, no cooperation occurs and the animal fails to learn
